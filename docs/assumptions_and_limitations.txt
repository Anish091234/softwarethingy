1. TOOL CLASSIFICATION
================================================================================

LunaRad is a conceptual design and early-stage research tool for
estimating radiation shielding effectiveness of lunar habitat wall
configurations. It is intended to support architectural trade studies,
material down-selection, and preliminary dose estimation during the
conceptual design phase of lunar surface habitats.

LunaRad is NOT:
  - A Monte Carlo radiation transport solver
  - A replacement for PHITS, GEANT4, FLUKA, or MCNP
  - A deterministic transport solver (e.g., HZETRN)
  - A certified tool for crew dose-limit compliance assessment

LunaRad IS:
  - An OLTARIS-inspired conceptual workflow tool
  - A rapid screening tool for comparing wall configurations
  - A framework for integrating published dose-depth data
  - A design aid for identifying promising shielding strategies

================================================================================
2. RELATIONSHIP TO OLTARIS
================================================================================

LunaRad draws architectural inspiration from NASA's On-Line Tool for
the Assessment of Radiation in Space (OLTARIS) in its workflow of:
  (a) defining a radiation environment,
  (b) specifying a shielding geometry,
  (c) performing directional ray tracing, and
  (d) computing dose metrics behind shielding.

However, LunaRad does NOT reproduce OLTARIS results. Key differences:
  - OLTARIS uses HZETRN deterministic transport; LunaRad uses
    empirically parameterized dose-depth functions.
  - OLTARIS performs actual particle transport through materials;
    LunaRad applies areal-density-based attenuation approximations.
  - OLTARIS has been validated against flight data and Monte Carlo codes;
    LunaRad is validated only against published literature curves.

================================================================================
3. SHIELDING ANALYSIS METHOD
================================================================================

3.1 Areal-Density-Based Approximation
--------------------------------------
The core shielding method converts material path lengths to areal densities
(g/cm^2) and applies empirically derived dose-depth response functions.
For each ray direction, the transmitted dose is estimated as:

    D(x) = D_0 * f(x)

where:
    D_0  = unshielded dose or dose equivalent
    x    = total areal density traversed (g/cm^2)
    f(x) = empirical dose-depth function fit to published transport data

The dose-depth functions are parameterized separately for:
  - GCR dose (absorbed)
  - GCR dose equivalent
  - SPE dose (absorbed)
  - SPE dose equivalent

3.2 Key Simplifications
------------------------

(a) 1D Transport Approximation Along Each Ray
    - Each ray is treated independently; there is no lateral particle
      transport or cross-talk between adjacent rays.
    - The material stack along each ray is treated as a 1D slab problem.
    - This is equivalent to the "straight-ahead" approximation used in
      some deterministic codes but without actual particle-by-particle
      transport.

(b) Exponential Attenuation Model with Empirical Parameters
    - Primary particle attenuation follows an exponential-like decay
      modified by empirical correction factors.
    - Parameters are fit to published dose-depth curves from HZETRN,
      PHITS, or GEANT4 calculations in the literature.
    - The functional form is:
        f(x) = A * exp(-alpha * x) + B * exp(-beta * x) + C
      where A, B, C, alpha, beta are material-dependent empirical
      parameters. The second exponential and constant term approximate
      secondary particle buildup and equilibrium contributions.

(c) Quality Factors from ICRP 60/103
    - Radiation quality factors (Q) or radiation weighting factors (w_R)
      are taken from ICRP Publication 60 (1991) and ICRP Publication 103
      (2007).
    - Quality factors are applied as multiplicative factors to convert
      absorbed dose to dose equivalent.
    - The LET-dependent Q(L) function from ICRP 60 is used when
      LET spectra are available; otherwise, effective quality factors
      from the literature are applied.

(d) No Electromagnetic Cascade Simulation
    - Bremsstrahlung, pair production, and electromagnetic shower
      development are not explicitly modeled.
    - For the energies and materials relevant to GCR/SPE shielding
      (protons, HZE ions through low-Z to moderate-Z materials), this
      omission has limited impact on total dose estimates.
    - Users working with high-Z shielding materials (e.g., lead,
      tungsten) should be aware that electromagnetic cascade effects
      become significant and results will be less reliable.

(e) Approximate Secondary Neutron Buildup
    - Secondary neutron production and buildup behind shielding is
      approximated using empirical buildup factors rather than explicit
      neutron transport.
    - The buildup factor is parameterized as a function of areal density
      and primary particle type, fit to published transport calculations.
    - This approximation captures the general trend of neutron buildup
      but does not reproduce the detailed energy spectrum of secondary
      neutrons.

(f) No Nuclear Fragmentation Transport (Empirical Correction Only)
    - Nuclear fragmentation of HZE ions is not explicitly transported.
    - Instead, an empirical fragmentation correction factor is applied
      to account for the dose contribution of lighter fragments.
    - This correction is derived from published HZETRN and Monte Carlo
      fragmentation studies.
    - The correction captures the bulk effect but does not track
      individual fragment species or their separate dose contributions.

4. APPLICABILITY RANGE
================================================================================

4.1 Wall Thickness
    - Valid range: 0 to 100 g/cm^2 areal density
    - For aluminum: 0 to ~37 cm wall thickness
    - For regolith (rho ~ 1.5 g/cm^3): 0 to ~67 cm thickness
    - For PEEK (rho ~ 1.3 g/cm^3): 0 to ~77 cm thickness
    - Beyond 100 g/cm^2, the empirical parameterizations have not been
      validated and extrapolation warnings are issued.

4.2 Radiation Environments
    - GCR: Badhwar-O'Neill model, solar modulation parameter
      phi = 400-1200 MV (covering solar maximum to solar minimum)
    - SPE: Band-function spectral fits to historical events
      (Oct 1989, Sep 1989, Aug 1972, Jan 2005, and others)
    - Energy range: 10 MeV/n to 50 GeV/n for GCR ions;
      10 MeV to 10 GeV for SPE protons

4.3 Particle Species
    - GCR: Protons (Z=1) through Iron (Z=26)
    - SPE: Protons only (dominant species)
    - Secondary neutrons: empirical buildup approximation
    - Light ions (Z=1-2 fragments): included in fragmentation correction
    - Heavier fragments: lumped into empirical correction

4.4 Shielding Materials
    - Parameterized materials: aluminum, polyethylene, water, regolith
      (JSC-1A analog), PEEK, carbon fiber composites
    - Other materials: estimated via effective-Z and density scaling
      (reduced accuracy)
    - Multi-layer stacks: areal densities summed along each ray

================================================================================
5. KNOWN LIMITATIONS AND DIVERGENCE CONDITIONS
================================================================================

5.1 Conditions Where Results May Diverge from Full Transport
    - Thick high-Z shielding (>20 g/cm^2 of iron, tungsten, lead):
      electromagnetic cascade and neutron production not captured.
    - Very thin shielding (<1 g/cm^2): near-surface dose enhancement
      and delta-ray equilibrium effects not modeled.
    - Complex multi-layer geometries with vacuum gaps: interface
      effects at material boundaries not captured.
    - Energies below 10 MeV/n: nuclear interaction cross-sections
      change rapidly; empirical fits may not track.
    - Unusual material compositions not in the parameterization
      database: effective-Z scaling introduces additional uncertainty.

5.2 Expected Accuracy
    - GCR dose equivalent behind aluminum (5-50 g/cm^2):
      within 20-30% of HZETRN/OLTARIS results
    - SPE dose behind aluminum (5-50 g/cm^2):
      within 15-25% of transport code results
    - GCR dose behind regolith (5-50 g/cm^2):
      within 25-35% (fewer validation data points)
    - Relative ranking of shielding configurations:
      generally reliable for configurations within the validated range
    - Absolute dose values: should be treated as estimates with
      the stated uncertainty bounds

5.3 What This Tool Cannot Do
    - Cannot determine crew dose-limit compliance (use certified tools)
    - Cannot optimize shielding for specific organ doses
    - Cannot model active shielding (magnetic/electrostatic)
    - Cannot model time-dependent exposure during EVA
    - Cannot replace Monte Carlo simulations for final design

================================================================================
6. RECOMMENDED USE CASES
================================================================================

APPROPRIATE:
  - Comparing relative shielding effectiveness of wall configurations
  - Screening regolith/PEEK composite designs before detailed analysis
  - Estimating order-of-magnitude dose reduction for trade studies
  - Identifying promising configurations for follow-up transport analysis
  - Educational demonstrations of shielding principles

NOT APPROPRIATE:
  - Final design verification for crew radiation protection
  - Regulatory compliance assessment
  - Detailed organ dose estimation
  - Analysis of shielding outside the validated parameter range
  - Replacement for mission-specific radiation analysis
